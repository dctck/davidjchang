The Problem

The Challenge

Users were frustrated with our wallet's complex navigation requiring multiple screens to complete basic crypto transactions. The deeper the coin was buried in our hierarchy, the more steps users had to take.

User Feedback

"It takes too many steps just to send ENJ (crypto currency)"

"Switching between multiple wallets and addresses is confusing"

"Why do I have to dig into each coin just to send or receive?"

The Navigation Problem

Our previous structure was hierarchical but cumbersome:

Wallets (collapsible groups)
Addresses (grouped by networks like ETH, BTC)
Coin Details (for multi-currency networks like Ethereum)
Action Selection (Send/Receive)

While this organization made technical sense, it created unnecessary friction for the most common user actions.

Research & Discovery

UX SOLUTION

Design Explorations

Challenge

How do we show the right information first after users tap Send or Receive?

Solution: Coin-first selection flow

  1. 1. Select cryptocurrency type (narrows available networks)
  2. 2. Choose recipient address (filtered by network compatibility)
  3. 3. Enter amount

This approach aligned with industry best practices and reduced cognitive load.
